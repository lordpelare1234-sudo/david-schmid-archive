University at Buffalo Experts Available to Discuss the Solar Eclipse

The science and fascination behind the 2024 total solar eclipse

The Solar Eclipse Event

On April 8, 2024, a total solar eclipse crossed North America. Millions of people witnessed the moon completely block the sun, creating a rare moment of total darkness during daytime.

Scientific Importance

Solar eclipses allow scientists to study the sun’s outer atmosphere, known as the corona. During totality, astronomers can observe structures normally hidden by sunlight.

Public Interest

Cities along the path of totality experienced large tourism surges as people traveled to witness the event. Schools, universities, and observatories hosted viewing events.

Expert Commentary

Universities such as the University at Buffalo made experts available to discuss the eclipse, explaining both the scientific and cultural importance of the phenomenon.
